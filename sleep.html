Beauty Sleep 101

Melatonin, magnesium, blue light, sunlight? Learn how to determine your ideal sleep schedule, plus the wellness trends that actually work when it comes to optimizing your shut-eye.

January 2, 2018

Blue & Purple Fluid Detail by Mark Chadwick

I know, I know. The idea that sleep is essential to your health isn’t at all new — its benefits are well documented, and you can feel its obvious physical and mental effects when you have (or haven’t) had enough of it. And yet, we’re still struggling with sleep in a lot of ways — whether it’s getting enough quality sleep every night, or the elusive idea of a 10pm bedtime. So how can you optimize your sleep-wake cycle? Keep reading to find out.

You know when you say you’re going to go to bed at a reasonable hour, but end up watching Netflix until 2am for no good reason? It’s called bedtime procrastination, and it’s a very real phenomenon. Bedtime procrastination is basically the result of low self-regulation skills — interestingly, it’s thought to involve the same mental process as failing to resist eating a cupcake when you’re dieting [1]. So how can you combat the inherent impulse to watch the next episode of Black Mirror under the covers? Try setting a nightly bedtime alarm — it will act as a cue to snap you out of the foggy mental state that keeps you procrastinating.
